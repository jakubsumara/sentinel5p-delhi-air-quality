## Why Do We See Both?

Different months have different wind conditions:

1. **Winter months (Nov-Feb):** Often have low wind speeds â†’ **Red circles (Local)**
   - Temperature inversions trap pollution
   - Calm conditions allow local emissions to accumulate

2. **Summer/Monsoon months (Mar-Oct):** Often have higher wind speeds â†’ **Blue squares (Advected)**
   - Stronger winds transport pollution
   - Regional circulation patterns bring pollution from surrounding areas

## Why Are the Values Similar?

This is actually a **valid scientific finding**! Here's why:

### 1. **Delhi Has High Baseline Pollution**
- Even on "local" days, there's significant pollution from:
  - Traffic
  - Industry
  - Power plants
  - Construction

### 2. **Monthly Averaging Smooths Differences**
- Daily variations are averaged out
- Extreme events (very high or very low) get smoothed
- Monthly means tend to converge

### 3. **Both Regimes Contribute**
- **Local days:** High pollution from local sources
- **Advected days:** High pollution from transported sources
- Result: Similar overall levels, but different **sources**

### 4. **The Classification is About SOURCE, Not LEVEL**
- Red = "This pollution likely came from Delhi itself"
- Blue = "This pollution likely came from outside Delhi"
- Both can have high concentrations!

## Key Insight

The classification tells us **WHERE pollution comes from**, not just **HOW MUCH** there is:

- **Local regime:** "Delhi's own pollution"
- **Advected regime:** "Pollution brought in from elsewhere"

Both can be high! The important finding is:
- **62.5% of months** = Local pollution dominant
- **37.5% of months** = Transported pollution dominant

This shows Delhi's pollution is a mix of local and regional sources.
